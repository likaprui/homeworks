The Rich History and recipie of Margherita Pizza: A Culinary Classic

Discover the captivating journey of one of Italy's most iconic dishes — the Margherita Pizza . With its simple yet exquisite combination of fresh ingredients, this pizza has transcended time and borders to become a beloved culinary classic around the globe.

The Margherita Pizza traces its roots back to 1889 in Naples, Italy . Legend has it that this masterpiece was created in honor of Queen Margherita of Savoy by renowned pizzaiolo Raffaele Esposito. The story goes that Esposito crafted a pizza featuring the colors of the Italian flag _ tomatoes (red), mozzarella (white), and basil (green) _ symbolizing the unity of the newly unified Italy. The recipe traditionally includes a thin crust, San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella cheese, extra-virgin olive oil, and aromatic basil leaves. The combination of these high-quality, flavorful ingredients creates a harmonious blend that captivates the palate.
Whether you're a pizza aficionado or a newcomer to this culinary masterpiece, indulge in the rich history and irresistible taste of the Margherita Pizza—a dish that continues to bring people together across continents and cultures.

Ingredients Quantity
wheat pizza dough 1 batch
San Marzano tomatoes 1 cup tomato souce
fresh Mozzarella cheese 8 ounces
Fresh basil leaves Handful
Olive oil fresh
Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 500 degrees Fahrenheit with the rack in the upper third of the oven. If you are using a baking stone or baking steel, place it on the top rack. Prepare the dough in step 5.
  2. Place a medium sized bowl in the sink and pour the canned tomatoes, juices and all into the bowl. Chop tomatoes by hand. Spread about ¾ cup tomato sauce evenly over each pizza, leaving about 1 inch around the bare edges.
  3. If your mozzarella is packed in water, drain the water and gently pat the mozzarella dry on a clean tea towel or paper towels. If you are working with large mozzarella balls, cut them into smaller 1-inch balls. Spread the mozzarella over the pizza, putting a little extra in the center of the pizza as it melts towards the edges.
  4. Bake the pizzas individually on the top rack until the crust is golden and the cheese is just golden, about 10 to 12 minutes (or significantly less if you're using a baking stone/steel – keep an eye on it).
  5. Top each pizza generously with fresh basil, followed by a drizzle of olive oil, salt and red pepper flakes, if desired. Cut and enjoy. Leftover pizza keeps well in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
